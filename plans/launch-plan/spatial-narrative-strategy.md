# Spatial Narrative Strategy: /launch Experience

> Copy system for the Safetrekr spatial ZUI ring at `/launch`.
> Voice: Calm Authority. Vocabulary: operational, documented, verified.
> Target: Security directors, safety officers, risk managers (40-60, liability-focused).

---

## 1. Discovery Narrative Arc

### The Question They Arrive With

> "Can I trust this platform with my travelers' safety — and can I prove I did my due diligence?"

Every person who reaches `/launch` carries a version of this question. They are not browsing. They are evaluating. They have either experienced an incident that exposed their liability, or they are trying to prevent one. They are looking for evidence — not promises, not animations, not marketing language. Evidence.

### What They Must Believe Before They Act

The spatial ring is a persuasion sequence disguised as navigation. Each card moves the user through a belief ladder:

| Stage | Belief Required | Card That Delivers It |
|-------|----------------|----------------------|
| 1. Recognition | "These people understand my world." | The aesthetic itself (dark, operational, no stock photos) |
| 2. Comprehension | "I understand what this system does." | **How It Works** |
| 3. Relevance | "This was built for my exact situation." | **Who It's For** |
| 4. Depth | "The technology is real and substantial." | **Platform** |
| 5. Trust | "My IT and legal teams will approve this." | **Security** |
| 6. Feasibility | "We can afford this. The math works." | **Pricing** |
| 7. Credibility | "I know who stands behind this." | **About Us** |
| 8. Action | "The next step is safe and easy." | **Get Started** |

### The Natural Discovery Order

The ring reads clockwise from 12 o'clock. This is intentional. The sequence mirrors how institutional buyers evaluate:

```
              How It Works          (What is it?)
                   |
    Get Started    |    Who It's For (Is it for me?)
         \         |         /
          \        |        /
           \   [HUB GLYPH]  /     Platform     (How deep is it?)
          /                  \
         /                    \
    About Us                Security  (Can I trust it?)
         \                    /
          \                  /
              Pricing        (Can I afford it?)
```

The user's eye lands on the center hub (thesis statement), radiates outward to the top card (How It Works), then sweeps clockwise through the belief ladder. Get Started sits at 10 o'clock — the last card encountered clockwise, the natural conclusion.

### The Aha Moment

The aha moment is the independent analyst review. It first appears in How It Works ("Every trip reviewed by an independent safety analyst before departure"), deepens in Platform ("Analyst Portal — 18-dimension risk assessment"), and is reinforced in About Us ("Who reviews your trips?"). By the time they reach Get Started, they have internalized the one thing no competitor can claim: *this is not just software — someone with professional safety credentials reviews every trip before your people leave*.

### Three-Act Structure

**ACT 1 — ARRIVAL (0-3 seconds)**
The dark canvas activates. The ring emerges with staggered entrance animation. Ambient panels glow faintly with operational data. The system status panel reads "ALL CLEAR." The top telemetry bar reads "SAFETREKR // MISSION CONTROL." First impression: *this is operational. This is serious. This is different.*

**ACT 2 — ORIENTATION (3-10 seconds)**
The onboarding nudge appears as ambient text (not a modal). The user's eye settles on the center hub — the thesis statement — then radiates to the ring. The ambient feed shows safety-relevant activity verbs (ROUTE.SCORED, ANALYST.CLEARED, CHECKIN.CONFIRMED). The subliminal message: *this platform is alive and working right now.*

**ACT 3 — EXPLORATION (10+ seconds)**
The user begins clicking. Each card hover-reveal provides just enough to decide whether to dive in. The clockwise flow creates a natural persuasion arc: understand, identify, evaluate, trust, budget, commit. Every click takes them to a full content page (server-rendered, SEO-indexed) where the real depth lives. The spatial ring is the menu, not the meal.

## 3. Onboarding Micro-Copy

These strings appear as monospace ambient text, integrated into the spatial canvas — not as modals, tooltips, or overlays. They should feel like the interface acknowledging the user, the way a command center terminal would.

### First Load Nudge (appears immediately, fades after 4 seconds)

```
STATION ONLINE // SCROLL TO ZOOM / DRAG TO PAN
```

**Implementation:** Render as a monospace text element below the ring (world-space coordinates, approximately y: +520). Use the ghost text style (`rgba(var(--ambient-ink-rgb), 0.20)`). Fade in over 800ms, hold for 3 seconds, fade out over 1 second. `pointer-events: none`.

### Idle Nudge (appears if no interaction for 5 seconds)

```
AWAITING INPUT // SELECT A STATION TO BEGIN
```

**Implementation:** Same position and style as above. Only triggers once. Fades in over 600ms, persists until the user interacts, then fades out over 500ms. Slightly brighter than the first nudge (`rgba(var(--ambient-ink-rgb), 0.25)`).

### First Interaction Acknowledgment (appears on first pan or zoom)

```
NAVIGATION ACTIVE
```

**Implementation:** Brief flash of text in the same position. Appears for 1.5 seconds with a subtle green tint (`rgba(var(--healthy-rgb), 0.30)`), then fades. This is the system confirming it received your input — a micro-moment of reciprocity.

## 7. Copy Validation Against Brand Rules

| Rule | Status | Evidence |
|------|--------|---------|
| Never lead with "peace of mind" | PASS | Not used anywhere. Emotional register stays operational. |
| Operational vocabulary | PASS | verified, documented, reviewed, monitored, scored, archived, assigned, cleared |
| Connect to analyst review | PASS | Appears in How It Works (primary), Platform (secondary), About (tertiary), Pricing (proof) |
| Address liability anxiety | PASS | "Did you do everything you could?" reflected in hub thesis and Who It's For tagline |
| Low-commitment conversion | PASS | "20-minute briefing, no commitment required" — respectful, specific, no-pressure |
| No superlatives or buzzwords | PASS | No "seamless," "revolutionary," "AI-powered," "game-changing" |
| Monospace for metadata | PASS | All stat lines are monospace operational data |
| Specific over vague | PASS | "18 dimensions" not "comprehensive review"; "$450/$750/$1,250" not "affordable pricing" |
