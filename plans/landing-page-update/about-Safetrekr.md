Safetrekr – Trip-Centric Safety & Emergency Preparedness

Safetrekr is a trip safety platform built for schools, churches, mission organizations, and youth programs. It brings together trip planning, risk intelligence, safety workflows, and mobile apps for chaperones, travelers, and guardians—so everyone knows what to do if something goes wrong.

Instead of spreadsheets, email threads, and group chats, Safetrekr gives you one place to:
	•	Design and approve trips.
	•	Configure Safety and Emergency Preparedness.
	•	Run trips in the field with clear playbooks.
	•	Capture the evidence that you did the right things.

⸻

How It Works – The Safetrekr Suite

Safetrekr is made up of three main web portals and a traveler-side app that all talk to the same brain.

1. Client Portal – For Trip Owners & Org Admins

Who uses it: Teachers, group leaders, trip coordinators, church staff, athletic directors.

What it does:
	•	Trip Creation & Submission
Build trips in a guided flow: basics, itinerary, lodging, transportation, rosters, documents.
	•	Safety & Emergency Preparedness (Read-Only)
See the Analyst-authored safety plan for each trip:
	•	Rally Points & Safe Houses
	•	Local emergency services
	•	Primary/backup medical facilities
	•	Evacuation & relocation strategy
	•	First-aid / medical kit profile
	•	System recommendations based on region & response times
	•	Trip Status & Packets
Track trip status (Draft → Submitted → In Review → Approved) and download:
	•	Guardian packets (reassurance-oriented)
	•	Chaperone packets (operational detail)
	•	Org-Level Controls
Manage users and roles, see which trips are active, and (optionally) control features like FX display and current weather.

The Client Portal lets orgs see and align with a professional safety & emergency plan—without having to design it themselves.

⸻

2. Analyst Portal – For Safetrekr Reviewers & Safety Teams

Who uses it: Safetrekr analysts, HQ safety/risk staff.

What it does:
	•	Tiered Review Queue & Assignment
Trips auto-classified (Tier 1/2/3) and placed into pools. Analysts claim trips (or are directly assigned) based on complexity and capacity.
	•	Trip Review Workbench (3-Column Layout)
Fifteen (and growing) sections with:
	•	Left: Section navigation with completion status.
	•	Center: Section content (Overview, Participants, Lodging, Itinerary, Safety, Emergency Preparedness, Documents, Issues, Packet, Approval).
	•	Right: Checklist & comments (Required/Optional/Comments tabs).
	•	Safety & Emergency Preparedness Configuration
Analysts configure and validate:
	•	Rally Points & Safe Houses and coverage.
	•	Critical contacts (leaders, org, local emergency numbers, hospitals, embassies).
	•	Primary & backup medical facilities, with response-time awareness.
	•	Shelter-in-place, relocation triggers, and “where we go if we have to leave.”
	•	Medical profile (env type, medic/no medic) & kit profile.
	•	System recommendations informed by risk intel.
	•	Issues & Comms + Approval Gating
Unresolved issues and external-facing comments are bundled into Issues & Comms, summarized for the client, and used to gate:
	•	Final Approval / Request Revision / Reject decision.
	•	A clear, signed record that the trip was reviewed and approved under defined safety criteria.

The Analyst Portal is where “duty of care” is turned into structured, reviewable, and auditable work.

⸻

3. HQ / Safetrekr Admin Portal – For System-Wide Control

Who uses it: Safetrekr HQ, operations, and sys-admin.

What it does:
	•	System Overview
KPIs across all orgs:
	•	Total orgs, trips, trips in review/approved.
	•	System health for core services (risk intel, FX, weather).
	•	Organization Management
List & manage organizations:
	•	Status (active/suspended/trial), plan tier, usage.
	•	Basic settings (FX/Weather enabled, notification defaults).
	•	Assignment & Policy Controls
	•	Configure tiered pools and analyst capabilities.
	•	Manage trip assignment: pools vs direct analyst assignment.
	•	View and adjust global feature flags/policies.

The HQ portal gives Safetrekr staff the levers to manage growth, quality, and safety standards at scale.

⸻

4. Traveler App – For Chaperones, Travelers & Guardians

Who uses it: Chaperones, travelers (students, participants), and guardians/parents.

What it does:

Chaperone View
	•	Today View:
Current and next event, Rally Point, headcount summary, quick actions.
	•	Check-ins & Musters:
Run headcounts; mark travelers present/missing/excused.
	•	Alerts:
Trip-filtered safety alerts with concrete COAs (“Stay in place”, “Move to Rally Point East”, “Relocate to [Safe House]”).
	•	Safety & Emergency:
Rally Points, Safe Houses, Emergency Contacts, simplified version of the Emergency Preparedness plan.

Traveler View
	•	Today & Schedule:
Where to be, when, and how to get there (lightweight, low-friction).
	•	Packet & Help:
Trip overview, basic safety guidance, and “who do I call if I’m lost or worried?”

Guardian View
	•	Trip & Readiness Summary:
Child’s trip, status of consents & docs, high-level itinerary.
	•	Emergency Preparedness Summary:
Calm explanation of the plan and who’s in charge.
	•	Appropriate Alerts:
“Group is safe and sheltering” vs. raw incident feed.

The Traveler App keeps field operations and communication grounded in the same plan that was reviewed and approved—without overwhelming users.

5. Under the Hood – Risk & Context

Behind all of this:
	•	safetrekr-core manages data, auth, and APIs.
	•	TarvaRI ingests and scores risk (weather, seismic, unrest, health, infrastructure) and suggests safe routes, Rally Points, Safe Houses, and risk summaries.
	•	FX and weather integrations give travelers and reviewers helpful context (“1 USD ≈ local currency”, “Current weather at the trip location”).
